About

One profile, one message.

This site mirrors the same core story behind HostCell: I am a bioengineer and programmer working where upstream bioprocessing, molecular biology, and software development meet.

I am a Bioengineer and PhD Candidate in Biochemical Sciences at Instituto de Biotecnología, UNAM, working under Dr. Tonatiuh Ramírez. My work spans upstream bioprocessing with CHO, S. cerevisiae, and E. coli systems; molecular biology; and software development for scientific workflows.

Many of the tools I build come from repetitive, high-stakes calculations in a PhD routine where a single arithmetic mistake can cost days of culture work. What begins as a local script often becomes a browser-based application designed to reduce human error and improve reproducibility.

Beyond research, I served for more than three years as Coordinator of the Mexican Bioimaging Workshops, a collaborative initiative supported by the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative, Leica, UNAM, and CONACYT. That experience shaped a lasting conviction: access to good tools is part of scientific access itself.
