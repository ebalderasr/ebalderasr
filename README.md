## About

I am a **Bioengineer and PhD Candidate in Biochemical Sciences at Instituto de Biotecnología, UNAM**, working under [Dr. Tonatiuh Ramírez](https://orcid.org/0000-0001-9005-3496). My work sits at the intersection of **upstream bioprocessing**, **molecular biology**, and **software development for scientific workflows**.

Across CHO, *S. cerevisiae*, and *E. coli* systems, I work on the kind of experimental routines where one spreadsheet mistake can cost days of culture time. That is why many of my projects turn repetitive, high-stakes laboratory calculations into **reliable, reproducible, and browser-based tools**.

Beyond the bench, I served for more than three years as Coordinator of the **Mexican Bioimaging Workshops (MBW)**, a high-impact initiative supported by the **Chan Zuckerberg Initiative (CZI)**, Leica, UNAM, and CONACYT. That experience reinforced a core idea behind my work: **access to good tools is a scientific equity issue**.

## Featured Projects

### [HostCell](https://github.com/ebalderasr/HostCell)

My main public platform for bench-side scientific software. HostCell connects my bioengineering work with software design through a suite of offline-ready tools built to reduce human error in everyday lab operations.

### [Clonalyzer-2](https://github.com/ebalderasr/Clonalyzer-2)

A browser-based kinetics analysis app for CHO fed-batch cultures. It processes raw experimental data locally in the browser, computes kinetic parameters, generates plots, and packages results for downstream comparison.

### [ysi-processor](https://github.com/ebalderasr/ysi-processor)

A GitHub Pages app for **YSI 2950 BioSample** QC, replicate review, averaging, standard deviation reporting, and export-ready outputs. It keeps the analysis in the browser while preserving a tested Python engine behind the workflow design.

### [BioReact-Lite](https://github.com/ebalderasr/BioReact-Lite)

An interactive chemostat and bioreactor simulation tool for exploring Monod kinetics, reactor dynamics, and local stability analysis. It reflects the more computational side of my work in bioprocess modeling.
